The Myth

As a bodybuilder, your main goal is to fully stimulate the target muscle during your workout to add mass. When it comes to training legs, for years bodybuilders have believed the only way to gain size and full development of the leg muscles is to squat. Free bar squats needed to be the first exercises performed on your leg day, with heavy weight and a lot of sets. While free bar squats are a great movement, they are not optimal for everyone.

Optimal Training

At this stage I assume you've used the squat calculator to determine if free bar squatting is or isn't an optimal exercise for you. When creating a new training routing, exercise selection is very important. You should choose exercise that not only work towards your goal, but also work with your body type. Below you'll find a section for "optimal squatters" and "sub-optimal squatters". These sections contain exercises that will allow you to target and stimulate your legs as much as possible based on your body type.

Sub-optimal Squatter

Angled Leg Press

Key benefits

  • Abilty to change foot position to emphasize more quad or hamstring.
  • Great range of motion for taller athletes.

Demo

Hack Squat

Key benefits

  • Easier to maintain neutral spinal alignment.
  • Ability to change foot position to emphasize outer or inner quad development.
  • Less strain on the lower back.

Demo

Dumbbell Split Leg Squats

Key benefits

  • Using one leg at a time helps ensure equal strength and development.
  • Easier on the knee joints due to less weight needed.
  • Helps improve balance and stability.
