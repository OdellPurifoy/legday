The Goal

As a Powerlifter, strength is the name of the game. Many powerlifters feel they need to squat with the same technique as the other athletes. If a certain athlete has a big squat using high bar, others tend to feel they should high bar squat. The same tends to be true for sumo versus conventional deadlifts. While it is tempting to do what others have done to achieve success, it is extremely important to train in a manner that compliments your body. This will allow for better progress, lessen the chance of injury and reduce frustration.

Optimal Training

At this stage I assume you've used the squat calculator to determine if free bar squatting is or isn't an optimal exercise for you. When creating a new training routing, exercise selection is very important. You should choose exercise that not only work towards your goal, but also work with your body type. Below you'll find a section for "optimal squatters" and "sub-optimal squatters". These sections show the most optimal type of squat you should be doing based on your structure.

Optimal Squatter

If free bar squatting is optimal for your structure, high bar squatting may be optimal for you. Take a look at the benefits and demo below of some great exercises for leg development.

High Bar Squat

Key benefits:

  • Abilty to change foot position to emphasize more quad or hamstring.
  • Great range of motion for taller athletes.
  • Less strain on the lower back.
